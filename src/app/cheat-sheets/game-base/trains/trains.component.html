There are two systems for trains/stations:
  • Locomotive(s) on one end of the train. The train stops and leaves the station in the same direction. (Pass Through Station)
  • Locomotive(s) on both ends of the train. The train stops and leaves the station in the opposite direction. (Terminal Station)
Example station names and interrupts patterns:
  • or (load/unload)
  • or (fluid/cargo wagon)
  • [#locomotives in front]-[#wagons]-[#locomotives behind]
Example train configurations for an average game (locos on both ends of the train):
  • 2-4-2 trains, for ore.
  • 1-2-1 trains, for oil.
  • 1-1-1 trains, sulfuric acid for uranium ore mines.

Signals tutorial

The main rules for signalling are:
  • Chain signal before the intersection, but block signal after the intersection. "Chain in, block out."
  • Your longest train must fit between the signals after the intersection. This prevents deadlocks.

You must have at least one of each of these stations. Set the train limit to 1, or to the number of trains that fit in a stacker before that station.
  • Load Station, for mining outposts or parts production.
  • Unload Station, for the main base or final assembly of parts.
  • Fuel Station. Put this near your rocket fuel production.
