Space Platforms

  • Starting planet (Nauvis) has no asteroids to worry about, so stationary platforms there do not need defenses.
  • You can connect asteroid collectors to a circuit network to set filters for what they can grab so that your platform doesn't overflow on material.
  • Routes between the inner planets (Nauvis, Vulcanus, Gleba and Fulgora) have medium asteroids which damage your space platform. Build (and supply) defenses to break the asteroids into safe asteroid chunks.

  • While you cannot bring anything in your inventory directly, you can load up space platforms with items which can be dropped on your planet of choice - you do not need a landing pad.
    • You can craft items in space, like you can on the ground. For example: the rocket silo is too heavy for your rocket. But you can launch the items needed to build the rocket silo, and then craft the silo in space.

Vulcanus

Planet Start
  • Recommended items to bring to Vulcanus for faster bootstrapping:
  • Use robots (vs mining by hand) to gather rocks to get tungsten ore easily before getting access to patches
  • Rush the Foundry asap and set it as the building to build more Foundries; because it includes built in productivity, you will get a free Foundry for every 2 crafts!
  • You can use inserters to dump (destroy) items into lava (like excess stone).
  • 1 ore = 15 molten fluid

Fulgora

Planet Start
  • Recommended items to bring to Fulgora for faster bootstrapping:
  • Most islands on Fulgora are small, and recycling scrap gives you a lot of different items. You can use robots to sort the output. Use the items you get from recycling to craft starter items.
  • Setup filters on (12 recycled items from scrap) + 1 generic storage to start
  • Use to process recycling when using a logistics network.
Recycling
Recycler Scrap Percent Chance Ratios

  • When recycled, items return 25% of their normal ingredients.
  • Items that are made using an irreversible recipe (smelting, etc..) or have no recipe at all, (ores, etc..) will have a 25% chance of being returned, otherwise destroying the item.
  • The time it takes to recycle an item is 1/16th (0.0625) of the time it takes to craft that item, multiplied by recycler crafting speed.
  • Recycler (non moduled) takes in 2.5 scrap/s and spits out 1.47 items/s
